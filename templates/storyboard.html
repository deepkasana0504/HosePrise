Renovation Impact on Sales

Key Insight: The histogram below illustrates how sales are distributed based on years since renovation. Recently renovated homes (0-15 years) tend to fall in higher price brackets, while homes renovated over 30 years ago show lower average prices. This suggests renovations have a measurable positive impact on sale prices, with the effect diminishing over time.

House Age & Renovation Status

Distribution Analysis: The donut chart reveals how the housing inventory is distributed across different age groups. Understanding this distribution helps identify renovation opportunities — older homes that haven't been renovated represent potential for value addition, while the proportion of renovated vs. non-renovated homes informs market positioning strategy.

Features & House Age

Feature Patterns: This grouped bar chart reveals how house features (bedrooms, bathrooms, floors) vary with house age. Newer homes tend to have more bathrooms and modernized floor plans, while older homes may have more bedrooms but fewer bathrooms. These patterns guide renovation priorities and pricing models for different property types.

Conclusions & Recommendations

Summary: The analysis reveals several actionable insights for ABC Company:
Renovation ROI

Recently renovated homes command premium prices. Investments in renovation within the last 10 years show the strongest correlation with higher sale prices.

Market Opportunity

A significant portion of older homes remain unrenovated, representing a large opportunity for value-add renovations and targeted marketing.

Feature Preferences

Modern buyers prefer homes with 2-3 bathrooms and open floor plans. Older homes with more bedrooms should be marketed highlighting space potential.

Pricing Strategy

Pricing models should weight renovation recency, living area, and condition as primary factors. Price per sqft varies significantly between renovated and unrenovated homes.
